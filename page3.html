Recycling Basics

Recycling is the process of collecting and processing the materials that would otherwise be thrown away as trash and turning them into new products. Recycling can be better your community and the environment.

Some of the common products that we use which can be made from recycled content are

Egg Cartons
Cereal boxes
Comic books
Laundry detergent containers 
Newspapers
Paper towels
Aluminum cans
Trash bags

Reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills.

Engage our children!

 Teaching kids about recycling can be fun and rewarding, in every sense. Encouraging children to recycle is a way of teaching them about sustainability. Learning the importance of being sustainable shows them that the environment is important.

Imagine your great-grandchildren never setting foot on the beach because it’s too dangerous. That’s a sad thought.

Explain that certain materials don’t decompose and some will exist for hundreds if not thousands of years.

Schools can help teach good recycling habits too. If your local school doesn’t recycle, become (or nominate someone else!) an eco-warrior that looks after recycling.

Collect waste paper for wet playtime scribbles for example. Ask the kids where the waste from their packed lunches goes – recycling, composting or landfill bins.

Kids respond well to visual aids too. Zero waste YouTube clips are a great starting point for discussions about landfill and negative environmental impacts.
