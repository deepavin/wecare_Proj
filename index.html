Think Before you Trash It!

The world recycles just 14% of the plastic packaging that is used.

8m tons of plastic end up in the ocean each year where sea life and birds die from eating or get tangled in it.

We can save 28% of your overall household waste by composting our food scraps and yard waste.

Composting can lower our carbon footprint by contributing less waste to landfills

Over 75% of waste is recyclable, but we only recycle about 30% of it.

Recycling right will reduce Recycling contamination which means more reusable materials end up in the landfill.

How Much Trash Does the Average Person Produce per Year?

A Clean and Green City is Our Dream!!

No other country is drowning in waste as much as the USA

On a worldwide scale, we produce 2.6 trillion pounds of trash per year. 

According to the EPA, the average person in the U.S. produces 4.9 pounds of trash per day, as of 2020. That's equates to about 147 pounds of trash per person per month, or 1,788.5 pounds per year. That's up from 4.4 pounds per person each day in 2013.

People produce two kilograms more waste today than they did in 2019.
That is despite the pandemic increasing the use of many more disposable items, such as medical masks and Covid rapid tests.


So how much waste is produced by a single family ?

There are approximately 317 million people in the United States, divided that number by our estimate of the average 4 person family, so that we can find out the number of families in the U.S.

317 million divided by 4 equals out to be 79,250,000 families in the U.S.

Estimated amount of garbage one person produces a week is approximately 10 pounds.

So an average American family consists of 4 people produces
approximately 40 pounds of garbage in a week.
